About Gym Guidelines

Cardio

"Cardio," short for cardiovascular exercise, refers to any physical activity that increases your heart rate and promotes cardiovascular fitness. These exercises are crucial for maintaining a healthy heart and circulatory system. Typically, cardio activities involve rhythmic movements of large muscle groups over a sustained period, such as running, cycling, swimming, or walking.

Muscle training

"Muscle training," also known as strength or resistance training, involves exercises designed to strengthen and develop muscles through the use of resistance, such as weights, resistance bands, or body weight. The primary objective of muscle training is to increase muscle strength, endurance, and size by repeatedly contracting muscles against resistance.

Strength training

"Strength training," often referred to as "weight training" or "resistance training," is a form of exercise focused on building and toning muscles by using resistance. This resistance can come from various sources such as free weights (like dumbbells and barbells), weight machines, resistance bands, or even body weight exercises like push-ups and squats.
